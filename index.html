Welcome!

I'm interested in evidence-based international affairs, political economy, and economic development. My mission is to be a boundary spanner who translates research into informed decisions. See my research or view my full CV.

I am a Low Tuck Kwong Foundation Scholar in the Master in International Affairs at the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy, National University of Singapore (NUS). My interests center on international political economy, comparative politics, and economic development, with a focus on how states, markets, and institutions shape outcomes in developing economies. I am a Young Leader with the Pacific Forum Young Leaders Program and a reviewer for Jurnal Borneo Administrator (Indonesia’s State Administration Institution, LAN).

My training spans research methods, global political economy, international economic development, behavioral economics, comparative politics, and business–government relations. I have been educated across Singapore, Switzerland, the United Kingdom, Taiwan, and Indonesia.

I am currently pursuing the Master's in International Affairs at NUS (expected 2026) and currently a guest student at the University of St. Gallen (Fall 2025). I earned a B.A. in Political Science (International Relations) from Universitas Muhammadiyah Yogyakarta (2022), where I visited the University of Glasgow, School of Public Policy (Fall 2021) and Asia University, College of Humanities and Social Sciences (Fall 2020).

Outside work, I enjoy the outdoors (though I’m most often in the gym), and in my free time I play FPS and strategy games.

Latest Publication

I’m proud to co-author the AidData policy report “Balancing Risk and Reward: Who benefits from China’s investments in Indonesia?”, published on June 12, 2025.

The report decodes over two decades of Beijing’s state-backed development finance (nearly $70 billion from 2000–2023) and private FDI (over $94 billion from 2010–2024), revealing which actors truly benefit.

The report was covered by the South China Morning Post “Should Indonesia scrutinise China-funded projects? Report flags debt risk but also benefits” (July 16, 2025), which drew on our findings to discuss how Indonesia can maximise the benefits of Chinese investment while managing risks.
