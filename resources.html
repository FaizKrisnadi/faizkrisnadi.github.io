Trade, Disputes & Risk — quick visuals from my VIID 2025 paper

A five-slide tour: dispute timelines, policy-annotated trade trends, a non-WTO comparator, and how geopolitical risk (GPR) stretches filing lags. Swipe or use the arrows to explore.

DS456 / DS593 Eurostat / USITC UN Comtrade GPR index
Dispute timelines (consultations → panel → report)

DS456 (US–India, solar) progressed 2013–2016. DS593 (Indonesia–EU, palm oil) shows a long panel phase (2020–2025), consistent with pandemic-era disruption and a heavier docket.

IDN→EU HS1511 (Palm oil) with policy & WTO markers

Imports peaked mid-2010s, dipped 2015–2016, recovered by 2021, then softened as sustainability rules tightened (RED II 2018, 2019/807, EUDR 2023) while DS593 advanced.

PHL→CHN bananas (HS0803): 2012 maritime/SPS episode

Inspections/restrictions in Mar–Jun 2012 coincide with a sharp, short-run disruption. Manila did not file at the WTO; the issue resolved politically and trade later rebounded.

IND→USA solar (HS854140): trend + numbered events

DS456 adoption (Oct 2016) and India’s DCR removal (2017) precede US Section 201 safeguards (2018–2022/extension), which shape much of the later pattern.

Geopolitical Risk (GPR) vs filing/episode lags

Higher geopolitical risk coincides with longer lags between consultations and panel requests—and a longer episode in the non-WTO case. Exploratory pattern; small N.

Sources: WTO DS456 & DS593 dockets; EU legal acts (RED II, 2019/807, EUDR); UN Comtrade/FAO; USITC DataWeb; Caldara–Iacoviello GPR. See VIID paper for details.
