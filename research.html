Research

International Political Economy,
Chinese Capital in Indonesia,
and Sustainability.

I study how investment, industrial policy, and public narratives shape who benefits from cross-border capital in Indonesia. My current work focuses on Chinese development finance, foreign direct investment, and the politics of elite narratives.

The two pieces that define the current research agenda.

Featured Report Co-author

Balancing Risk and Reward: Who benefits from China’s investments in Indonesia?

AidData at William & Mary June 12, 2025 ~$164B tracked

Tracked nearly $70 billion in Beijing-backed development finance from 2000–2023 and over $94 billion in Chinese FDI from 2010–2024, showing which actors benefited from Chinese capital in Indonesia.
